# Retail Sales Exploratory Data Analysis

## Overview

This project presents an end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of a retail sales dataset containing 1,000 customer transactions. The objective is to transform raw sales data into meaningful business insights through data cleaning, statistical analysis, and visualization.

The project was completed as part of the **Oasis Infobyte Data Analytics Internship Program** and demonstrates the complete data analysis workflow using Python.

---

## Business Problem

Retail businesses rely on data to understand customer behaviour, monitor sales performance, and make informed business decisions. The objective of this project is to analyze historical retail transactions to answer questions such as:

- Which months and quarters generate the highest sales?
- Which product categories contribute the most revenue?
- How do customer demographics influence purchasing behaviour?
- What relationships exist between key business variables?
- What actionable recommendations can improve business performance?

---

## Objectives

- Perform data inspection and cleaning
- Generate descriptive statistics
- Analyze monthly and quarterly sales trends

## Dataset

The dataset contains **1,000 retail sales transactions** with the following attributes:

- Transaction ID
- Date
- Customer ID
- Gender
- Age
- Product Category
- Quantity
- Price per Unit
- Total Amount

## Key Findings

- Sales fluctuate throughout the year, with May recording the highest monthly revenue.
- Q4 generated the highest overall sales performance.
- Electronics and Clothing contributed the highest total revenue.
- Beauty products recorded the highest average order value despite lower sales volume.
- Customer purchases were almost evenly distributed between male and female customers.
- Product price showed the strongest positive correlation with total transaction value.
